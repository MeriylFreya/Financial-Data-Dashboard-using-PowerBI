# Financial Dataset Dashboard Analysis

## Overview

This project focuses on designing an interactive business dashboard using the Financial Sample dataset in Power BI. The objective of the dashboard is to help stakeholders quickly understand sales performance, profitability, product contribution, and market trends through clear visual storytelling.

The dashboard transforms raw financial data into meaningful business insights that support decision-making.

---

## Dataset Description

The Financial Sample dataset contains business transaction data across products, countries, and customer segments.

### Key Columns Used

- `Sales` – Revenue generated
- `Profit` – Net profit earned
- `Units Sold` – Number of units sold
- `Country` – Market location
- `Product` – Product name
- `Segment` – Customer/business segment
- `Month Name` – Sales month
- `Discounts` – Discounts provided

---

## Dashboard Components

### KPI Cards

The dashboard includes four key performance indicators:

- **Total Sales:** 118.73M
- **Total Profit:** 16.89M
- **Units Sold:** 1.13M
- **Profit Margin:** 0.14

These KPIs provide an instant snapshot of overall business performance.

---

## Visualizations Used

### 1. Monthly Sales Trend (Line Chart)

Shows how sales change across different months.

**Insight:**  
Sales peak strongly during October, December, and November, indicating strong seasonal demand in the final quarter.

---

### 2. Profit by Country (Horizontal Bar Chart)

Compares profit contribution across countries.

**Insight:**  
France and Germany generate the highest profits, while Mexico contributes comparatively less.

---

### 3. Segment Contribution (Donut Chart)

Shows revenue share by business segment.

**Insight:**  
Government and Small Business segments contribute the largest share of total sales.

---

### 4. Sales by Product (Bar Chart)

Ranks products based on sales performance.

**Insight:**  
Paseo is the top-performing product, followed by VTT and Velo.

---

### 5. Profit by Discount Band (Column Chart)

Analyzes relationship between profit and discounts.

**Insight:**  
Profit remains strong despite discounts, but excessive discounting should still be monitored to protect margins.

---

## Filters / Slicers Used

Interactive slicers were added for better user exploration:

- Country
- Product
- Segment

These allow stakeholders to dynamically analyze specific markets, products, or customer groups.

---

## Storytelling Summary

The business demonstrates strong revenue generation with total sales crossing 118 million. Profitability remains healthy, with over 16 million in profit and a positive margin.

Sales activity is strongest in the final quarter, especially October and December, suggesting seasonal buying behavior or year-end purchasing cycles.

From a geographic perspective, France and Germany are the most profitable markets, making them strategically important regions.

Product performance is concentrated around a few strong products, especially Paseo. This suggests that top products play a major role in driving total revenue.

Customer segments such as Government and Small Business dominate sales contribution, indicating where the business demand is strongest.

Overall, the company is performing well, with opportunities to improve margins, expand top-performing markets, and optimize lower-performing products.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Increase inventory and marketing efforts during peak months.
- Focus customer retention strategies in France and Germany.
- Promote top-performing products such as Paseo.
- Review discount strategies to maximize profitability.
- Explore growth opportunities in lower-performing regions.

## Interview Questions and Answers

### 1. What are the key elements of a dashboard?

A dashboard should include KPIs, charts, filters, clean layout, consistency, and actionable insights.

---

### 2. What is a KPI?

A KPI (Key Performance Indicator) is a measurable metric used to evaluate business performance, such as sales, profit, or growth.

---

### 3. What are slicers in Power BI?

Slicers are interactive filters that allow users to narrow dashboard data dynamically.

---

### 4. Difference between Power BI and Tableau?

Power BI is cost-effective and integrates well with Microsoft tools. Tableau is known for advanced visualization flexibility.

---

### 5. How do you make a dashboard interactive?

By using slicers, drill-through pages, tooltips, buttons, bookmarks, and cross-filtering visuals.

---

### 6. How do you deal with large datasets in dashboards?

Use data modeling, remove unnecessary columns, create summarized tables, optimize calculations, and use efficient relationships.

---

### 7. What chart types do you use for trend analysis?

Line charts, area charts, and combo charts are commonly used for trend analysis over time.
